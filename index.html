Glossary

EOS Management team: refers to the group of people specialized in the EOS methodology who provide advice to the organization during the implementation process.

Issues list: it is a key tool for systematically addressing and solving problems, maintaining a focus on continuos improvement and overcoming obstacles.

Leadership team: the leadership team is the select group of people who have the responsibility of making strategic decisions and leading the way towards the shared vision. This team usually includes the founders or owners of the company, as well as department managers and directors.

Learning unit: these units provide guidance and training to teams, helping them understand and effectively apply EOS concepts and tools to achieve organizational success.

Strategic objectives: strategic objectives are clear and specific goals that the company has set to achieve its long-term vision. These objectives are high-level and aligned with the organization's mission and values. Strategic objectives guide all company actions and decisions and help maintain focus on what really matters for . long-term success.

V/TO (the vision/traction organizer): strategic planning document that helps business leaders and their teams align with the company's vision, values, and long-term goals while providing a clear action plan to achieve them.
